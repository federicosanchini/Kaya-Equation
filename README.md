# Analysis of the Kaya Equation and the Impact of Renewable Energy on Environmental Sustainability

## Overview
This project presents a statistical analysis of the Kaya equation, focusing on the relationships between its main parameters: population, GDP per capita, energy intensity, and carbon intensity. It also explores how the share of renewable primary energy affects carbon intensity and environmental sustainability. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Objectives
The report aims to:
- analyze the mutual correlations among the Kaya equation parameters;
- identify which factors most strongly drive CO₂ emissions over time;
- examine the relationship between carbon intensity and renewable energy adoption;
- evaluate how renewable energy contributes to better environmental sustainability. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Methodology
The analysis is based on international datasets covering up to 165 countries over multiple decades. The study compares temporal trends in the Kaya parameters, evaluates their correlations, and investigates how changes in renewable energy shares are associated with changes in carbon intensity. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Main Findings
The results show that:
- GDP per capita is often the strongest driver of emissions growth;
- population and GDP per capita tend to be positively correlated;
- GDP per capita and energy intensity are generally negatively correlated;
- higher shares of renewable energy are associated with lower carbon intensity;
- increasing renewable energy production helps improve environmental sustainability. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Data Sources
The report uses public datasets on:
- Kaya equation parameters;
- renewable energy production;
- supporting literature on energy efficiency and emissions. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
